Polar decomposition

Mar 13, 20252 min read

Given a linear operator in a Hilbert space H\mathcal{H}H:

θ∈L(H)\theta \in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{H})θ∈L(H)

its polar decomposition is given by

θ=UJ=KU\theta = UJ = KUθ=UJ=KU

where UUU is a unitary operator and

J≡θ†θK≡θθ†J\equiv \sqrt{\theta^\dagger\theta}\qquad K\equiv\sqrt{\theta\theta^\dagger}J≡θ†θ​K≡θθ†​

are positive semidefinite operators

Proof

We prove the first identity, the second one follows

θ=UJ\theta = UJ θ=UJ

Expand JJJ in the spectral decomposition:

J≡θ†θ=∑iλi∣i⟩ ⁣⟨i∣J\equiv \sqrt{\theta ^\dagger \theta} = \sum _i \lambda _i \op{i}{i}J≡θ†θ​=i∑​λi​∣i⟩⟨i∣

In order to simplify the proof, assume λi>0\lambda_ i >0λi​>0, even though the result can also be proven for the general case λi≥0\lambda_ i \geq 0λi​≥0; then, define:

∣ψi⟩≡θ∣i⟩\ket{\psi_i} \equiv \theta \ket{i} ∣ψi​⟩≡θ∣i⟩ ⟨ψi|ψj⟩=θ†θ⟨i|θ†θ|j⟩=θ†θθ†θ⟨i|θ†θθ†θ|j⟩=λi2δij\ip{\psi _i}{\psi _j} = \matel{i}{\theta ^\dagger \theta}{j} =\matel{i}{\sqrt{\theta ^\dagger \theta}\sqrt{\theta ^\dagger \theta}}{j} = \lambda ^2 _i\delta_{ij}⟨ψi​∣ψj​⟩=θ†θ⟨i∣θ†θ∣j⟩=θ†θ​θ†θ​⟨i∣θ†θ​θ†θ​∣j⟩=λi2​δij​

This means that we can define a new orthonormal basis:

∣ei⟩≡1λi∣ψi⟩\ket{e _i} \equiv \frac{1}{\lambda _i}\ket{\psi _i}∣ei​⟩≡λi​1​∣ψi​⟩

This means that there exists a unitary transformation UUU such that:

∣ei⟩=U∣i⟩∀i\ket{e_i}= U\ket{i}\quad \forall i∣ei​⟩=U∣i⟩∀i U≡∑i∣ei⟩ ⁣⟨i∣U\equiv \sum_i \op{e_i}{i}U≡i∑​∣ei​⟩⟨i∣

Now we just need to prove that θ=UJ\theta = UJθ=UJ:

UJ=∑ij∣ei⟩ ⁣⟨i∣∣j⟩ ⁣⟨j∣λj=∑iλi∣ei⟩ ⁣⟨i∣UJ = \sum _{ij} \op{e_i}{i}\op{j}{j} \lambda_{j} = \sum _i \lambda _i \op {e_i}{i}UJ=ij∑​∣ei​⟩⟨i∣∣j⟩⟨j∣λj​=i∑​λi​∣ei​⟩⟨i∣

In order to obtain the needed result, we need to show that:

∣ψi⟩≡θ∣i⟩=UJ∣i⟩\ket{\psi _i} \equiv \theta \ket{i}= UJ \ket{i}∣ψi​⟩≡θ∣i⟩=UJ∣i⟩ UJ∣j⟩=∑iλi∣ei⟩ ⁣⟨i∣∣j⟩=λj∣ej⟩=∣ψj⟩UJ \ket{j} = \sum _i \lambda _i \op {e_i}{i}\ket{j} = \lambda_{j}\ket{e_j} = \ket{\psi _j} UJ∣j⟩=i∑​λi​∣ei​⟩⟨i∣∣j⟩=λj​∣ej​⟩=∣ψj​⟩

The second identity follows:

θ=UJU†U≡KU\theta = UJU^\dagger U\equiv K Uθ=UJU†U≡KU

where K≡UJU†≥0K \equiv UJU^\dagger \geq 0K≡UJU†≥0, we need to prove K=θθ†K = \sqrt{\theta \theta ^\dagger}K=θθ†​:

K=θU†K = \theta U^\daggerK=θU† KK=KK†=θθ†KK = KK^\dagger =\theta \theta ^\daggerKK=KK†=θθ†
